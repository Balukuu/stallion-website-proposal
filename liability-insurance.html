What is Liability Insurance?

As the legal landscape in Uganda matures, businesses are increasingly facing lawsuits from clients, employees, and members of the public. A simple slip-and-fall in your retail store or an error in your consultancy report can result in devastating financial judgements.

Liability insurance shields your business's balance sheet by covering the immense costs of legal defense, court settlements, and compensation payouts if your company is found legally responsible for injury, property damage, or financial loss to a third party.

What It Covers

Public Liability

Covers injury to members of the public or damage to their property occurring on your business premises.

Employers Liability (WC)

Statutory Workers Compensation for employees who suffer injury, disease, or death during the course of their employment.

Professional Indemnity

Crucial for consultants, doctors, and lawyers. Covers you against financial claims arising from professional negligence or bad advice.

Products Liability

Protects manufacturers and retailers if a defect in a product they sell causes harm or damage to a consumer.

Directors & Officers (D&O)

Covers company directors if they are personally sued for wrongful acts in managing the company.

Legal Defense Costs

Your policy pays for your lawyers and court fees, even if the third-party claim against your business is completely baseless.

Who is this for?

Professional Services: Law firms, accounting practices, IT consultants, and medical professionals working in Kampala who require Professional Indemnity.

Contractors & Builders: Who must prove they hold Contractors All Risk (CAR) and Public Liability cover before stepping onto major sites.

Retailers & Hospitality: Supermarkets, hotels, and restaurants who see high daily foot traffic and require robust Public Liability.
